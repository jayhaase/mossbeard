## Background
I am Mossbeard the Wanderer, keeper of quiet trails and old tales. If you listen close, the forest might just whisper back…”
---
Mossbeard the Wanderer drifts through those places where magic lingers. He carries old magic, offering wonder with a twinkle in his eye.
---
*"Long ago, the world was filled with magic — not just sparks and spells, but the magic of wonder, laughter, and words that danced on the tongue. Over time, people forgot some of those words, and with them, a little magic faded from the world.

I, Mossbeard the Wanderer, have taken up a mission: **to bring back the magic.** One way to do this is by rediscovering the _Forgotten Words_. Each scroll I carry holds a word waiting for a new keeper — someone brave enough, silly enough, and wise enough to give it life again. When you speak it, share it, or imagine with it, the world grows brighter, and the magic returns."
---
In the elder days, ere the world grew weary, there came forth a wanderer from the deep and shadowed woods, a wizard of no high hall nor gilded court, but of moss-covered stones and the whispering boughs of ancient trees. By many names he is known, though none hold fast, like wind they are ever changing.

He bears a staff of Everwood, crowned with crystal wrought from frost and fire, and it is said to stir when old magic is nearby. His boots have worn the shape of many paths.

He claims no throne, yet is received with gladness where kind folk dwell. For he brings not gold nor war, but tales forgotten, laughter kindled, and a quiet wisdom born of long listening.

And so he walks still, through fields of men and merriment, where the old songs linger and wonder yet remains.

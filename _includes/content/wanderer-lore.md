In the elder days, ere the world grew weary, there came forth a wanderer from the deep and shadowed woods — a wizard of no high hall nor gilded court, but of moss-covered stones and the whispering boughs of ancient trees. By many names he is known, though none hold fast; like wind they are ever changing.

He bears a staff of Everwood, crowned with heart of Fairy Wing Forest, said to stir when old magic is nearby. His boots have worn the shape of many paths.

He is received with gladness where kind folk dwell. For he brings not gold nor war, but tales forgotten, laughter kindled, and a quiet wisdom born of long listening.
